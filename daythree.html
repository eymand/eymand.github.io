Morning Session 1 - Peer Review in Scholarly Publishing

We'll do a deep dive into peer-review, looking at how it has evolved (and is still evolving!), and the many models of peer-review. We'll then take a closer look at the model we use at Kairos.

Morning Session 2 - Practicing Peer Review

In this session, we'll take a closer look at the Anti-Racist Review Heuristic as well as a number of reviewer guidelines for a variety of publications. We'll discuss our experiences of reviewing and being reviewed and articulate some best practices for operating on both sides of the review process.

Afternoon Session 1 - Practice Peer Review

We'll practice a couple of different models of peer review, from anonymous to collaborative. Given our limited time, we'll look at reviewing some conference proposals (rather than a full journal article) and we'll also look at a digital webtext.

Afternoon Session 2 - Peer Review Workflows

Based on the previous two sessions, we'll take some time to reflect on our peer review knowledge and experiences and collaboratively draft a document that can guide our workflow when we are asked to review others' scholarly work.
